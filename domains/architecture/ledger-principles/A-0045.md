# Summary

Exact double-entry posting rules for every loan event: origination (cash proceeds or asset-funded drawdown, with/without establishment fee), periodic interest accrual, payment (accrual-basis + cash-basis variants), payout (pro-rata accrual + principal + termination fee), write-off (debt forgiven), reversal, and correction. Each rule specifies accounts, amounts, tags, and the parent-child `transactions` / `acc_trans` shape.

The posting engine described here is the only path that writes ledger rows for loans — the loan service never writes `acc_trans` directly. It calls `journal.Post(ctx, cmd)` with a domain command, and the journal service applies this contract.

# Scope boundary — three layers

This document specifies the **GL accounting layer only**. Loan events produce outputs that are **not** in scope here:

1. **GL accounting layer** (this doc) — double-entry rows under AASB 9 amortised-cost measurement (simplified: simple interest on current principal balance).
2. **BAS / GST reporting layer** (forthcoming A-0042) — interest is generally input-taxed (s40-5 ITAA) so the interest leg does not flow to G10/G11/1A/1B. Establishment and termination fees may carry GST depending on the lender — those follow the normal BAS-extraction path via the source tax document. BAS labels are **not** tagged onto `acc_trans`.
3. **Income-tax layer** (forthcoming R-0073) — owns s8-1 deductibility, TR 95/25 apportionment for mixed-use borrowings, and interest-capitalisation rules. The `use_of_funds` tag on acc_trans rows is what the tax layer reads; this doc only writes it.

# Requirement Link

Implements the GL-posting portion of R-0063 per the ledger principles in A-0009 (double-entry, atomicity, idempotency, immutability). Consumed by T-0035 (payment), T-0036 (payout), T-0037 (accrual engine).

# Proposed Design

## 1. Account Resolution

Each loan carries three nominal accounts resolved at origination time and cached on the `loan_register` row:

- `liability_account_id` — where principal sits on the balance sheet (category L).
- `accrued_interest_account_id` — contra-revenue for accrued interest payable (category L — another liability).
- `interest_expense_account_id` — P&amp;L interest expense (category E).

Resolution order:

1. Explicit override on the loan row (user picked specific accounts during Originate).
2. Tenant system defaults (seeded per COA template: e.g. 2400 Loans, 2410 Accrued Interest Payable, 7500 Interest Expense).

Origination-time accounts:

- Bank account (if cash proceeds) — caller-supplied.
- Capital account (if asset-funded drawdown — e.g. the loan proceeds pay for a vehicle) — caller-supplied; mirrors R-0062's acquisition account.
- Loan Establishment Fee (expense, category E) — from tenant COA defaults.

Payout-time accounts:

- Early Termination Fee (expense, category E) — from tenant COA defaults.

Write-off-time accounts:

- Other Income — Debt Forgiven (category I, `reporting_category='other_income'`) — AASB 116 §68 analogue: gain on extinguishment of debt is not operating revenue. Routes to "other income" reporting subtotal.

## 2. Origination

**Inputs**: loan, `principal`, `receiving_account_id` (bank or capital account), `establishment_fee` (optional), `establishment_fee_bank_account_id` (optional).

Parent `transactions`: `type='loan_origination'`, `reference='LOAN-{loan_id}-ORIG'`.

### 2.1 Core origination rows

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `receiving_account_id` (bank OR capital) | Dr | `principal` | `loan_id={loan.id}` |
| 2 | `loan.liability_account_id` | Cr | `principal` | `loan_id={loan.id}`, `use_of_funds={loan.use_of_funds}` |

### 2.2 Establishment fee rows (if `establishment_fee > 0`)

Posted as a sibling transaction (`type='loan_establishment_fee'`) referencing the same loan via `source_transaction_id`. Keeping it separate lets BAS extraction treat the fee (which may be GST-inclusive) independently of the origination.

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F1 | Loan Establishment Fee (expense) | Dr | `establishment_fee` | `loan_id={loan.id}` |
| F2 | `establishment_fee_bank_account_id` | Cr | `establishment_fee` | `loan_id={loan.id}` |

**Invariants**: per-transaction `sum(Dr) == sum(Cr)`.

## 3. Interest accrual (periodic run)

**Inputs**: `period_start`, `period_end`, list of eligible active loans with their calculated per-loan amounts.

Parent `transactions`: `type='interest_accrual_run'`, `reference='LOAN-ACCRUAL-{period_end:YYYY-MM}'`, one parent per run.

Child `acc_trans` rows: two per eligible loan.

For each loan `l`:

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `l.interest_expense_account_id` | Dr | `l.calculated_accrual` | `loan_id={l.id}`, `run_id={run.id}`, `use_of_funds={l.use_of_funds}` |
| 2 | `l.accrued_interest_account_id` | Cr | `l.calculated_accrual` | `loan_id={l.id}`, `run_id={run.id}` |

**Calculation**: `accrual = l.current_balance × (l.interest_rate_annual_pct / 100) × days_in_period / 365`. Simple interest on current balance, not compounding mid-period. Post-accrual, `accrued_interest_balance += accrual`.

**Invariants**: per-loan `Dr == Cr`; run-level `Dr total == Cr total`.

**Mixed-use loans**: the full accrual posts to interest expense with `use_of_funds='mixed'` and `business_use_pct=...` tags. Apportionment is tax-time — R-0073 reads these rows and computes the deductible portion. The GL does not pre-filter.

## 4. Payment

### 4.1 Accrual-basis (default)

**Inputs**: loan, `payment_date`, `principal_amount`, `interest_amount`, `bank_account_id`. Total = principal + interest.

Preconditions:
- If `interest_amount > loan.accrued_interest_balance`, the service posts a catch-up accrual from the last accrual period-end (or origination if none) to `payment_date` in the **same DB transaction**, bringing `accrued_interest_balance` up to at least the interest_amount. The catch-up accrual is its own child under a dedicated parent `transactions` of `type='interest_accrual_adjustment'` linked via `source_transaction_id` to the payment.

Parent `transactions`: `type='loan_payment'`, `reference='LOAN-{loan_id}-PMT-{date}'`.

Child rows:

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | `loan.accrued_interest_account_id` | Dr | `interest_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true` |
| P2 | `loan.liability_account_id` | Dr | `principal_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true` |
| P3 | `bank_account_id` | Cr | `interest_amount + principal_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true` |

Post-payment:
- `loan.current_balance -= principal_amount`.
- `loan.accrued_interest_balance -= interest_amount`.

**Invariants**: `Dr total = Cr total = principal + interest`.

### 4.2 Cash-basis (tenant-opt-in)

When the tenant's config flag `loan_interest_basis='cash'`, interest is direct-to-expense on payment and accrual runs are skipped:

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| P1 | `loan.interest_expense_account_id` | Dr | `interest_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true`, `use_of_funds={loan.use_of_funds}` |
| P2 | `loan.liability_account_id` | Dr | `principal_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true` |
| P3 | `bank_account_id` | Cr | `interest_amount + principal_amount` | `loan_id`, `payment=true` |

Post-payment: `loan.current_balance -= principal_amount` only; `accrued_interest_balance` remains 0.

### 4.3 Reverse payment

Posts exact inverses of §4.1 or §4.2 rows under a new `type='loan_payment_reversal'` parent, with `source_transaction_id` pointing to the original payment.

## 5. Payout

**Inputs**: loan, `payout_date`, `principal_clearance` (typically = `current_balance`), `interest_clearance` (typically = `accrued_interest_balance` + pro-rata), `termination_fee` (optional), `bank_account_id`, `termination_fee_bank_account_id` (optional).

Parent `transactions`: `type='loan_payout'`, `reference='LOAN-{loan_id}-PAYOUT'`.

### 5.1 Pro-rata accrual (if any)

For accrual-basis tenants, if `payout_date > last_accrual_period_end`, the service computes pro-rata interest from `last_accrual_period_end` (or origination) to `payout_date` and adds it as §3-shape child rows under the same `loan_payout` parent.

### 5.2 Payout rows

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| O1 | `loan.accrued_interest_account_id` | Dr | total accrued interest cleared | `loan_id`, `payout=true` |
| O2 | `loan.liability_account_id` | Dr | principal cleared | `loan_id`, `payout=true` |
| O3 | `bank_account_id` | Cr | `interest + principal` | `loan_id`, `payout=true` |

### 5.3 Termination fee (if any)

Posted as sibling transaction (`type='loan_termination_fee'`, `source_transaction_id={payout_id}`) to keep BAS extraction clean (fee may be GST-inclusive).

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| F1 | Loan Termination Fee (expense) | Dr | `termination_fee` | `loan_id` |
| F2 | `termination_fee_bank_account_id` | Cr | `termination_fee` | `loan_id` |

Post-payout:
- `loan.current_balance = 0`.
- `loan.accrued_interest_balance = 0`.
- `loan.status = 'paid_out'`, `loan.payout_date = payout_date`, `loan.payout_transaction_id = parent`.

**Invariants**: payout-parent `Dr = Cr`; if on cash-basis, skip §5.1; O1 uses interest expense instead of accrued interest.

## 6. Write-off (debt forgiven)

**Inputs**: loan, `effective_date`, `reason` (audit payload).

Parent `transactions`: `type='loan_write_off'`, `reference='LOAN-{loan_id}-WRITEOFF'`.

| # | Account | Side | Amount | Tags |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| W1 | `loan.liability_account_id` | Dr | `current_balance` | `loan_id`, `write_off=true` |
| W2 | `loan.accrued_interest_account_id` | Dr | `accrued_interest_balance` | `loan_id`, `write_off=true` |
| W3 | Other Income — Debt Forgiven (`reporting_category='other_income'`) | Cr | `current_balance + accrued_interest_balance` | `loan_id`, `write_off=true` |

Post:
- `loan.current_balance = 0`, `accrued_interest_balance = 0`, `status='written_off'`.
- Tax assessability (debt-forgiveness rules) is an R-0073 tax-layer concern; the accounting journal is always the same.

## 7. Reversal of accrual run

Parent `transactions`: `type='interest_accrual_reversal'`, `source_transaction_id={original run}`. Rows are exact inverses of §3 rows for the affected loans. Reverts `accrued_interest_balance` on each loan.

## 8. Correction — four distinct paths

AASB 108 distinguishes **error correction** (retrospective or current-period) from **change in accounting estimate** (prospective). This contract mirrors the split.

### 8.1 Clerical edit (no GL)

Non-posting fields only — name, description, `use_of_funds` flag, `business_use_pct`. No `acc_trans` rows. Audit row with diff. Handled by A-0044 `EditLoanNonPosting`.

### 8.2 Same-period reclassification

Wrong liability account or wrong GL wiring caught in the open period. Two journals in one DB transaction:

1. Reversal of the origination rows (and any payments/accruals posted so far in the period). Parent `type='loan_reclass_reversal'`, `correction_id={uuid}`.
2. Fresh origination per §2 with corrected inputs. Parent `type='loan_origination'`, `correction_id={same uuid}`, `source_transaction_id={reversal id}`.

Period lock check: target period must be OPEN and not BAS-lodged. Handled by A-0044 `ReclassifyCurrentPeriod`.

### 8.3 Change in accounting estimate (prospective, no GL)

Change to `interest_rate_annual_pct` or `maturity_date` based on new information — typically a variable-rate adjustment or term extension. **This is NOT an error** — it is a revised estimate applied prospectively.

No reversal. No `acc_trans` rows. `loan.interest_rate_annual_pct` / `maturity_date` updated; future accrual runs + payment splits use the new values. `loan.estimate_changes[]` appended with `{field, old, new, effective_from, reason}`. Audit `estimate_changed`.

Handled by A-0044 `ChangeLoanEstimate`.

### 8.4 Prior-period restatement

Material error affecting a closed or BAS-lodged period. AASB 108 §41–49 requires retrospective restatement.

Constraints: requires `loans:correct` + `admin:restate_prior_period`; carries documented reason + reviewer sign-off in audit payload.

Parent `transactions`: `type='loan_prior_period_restatement'` in the **current** open period. Rows post correction to:

- `liability_account_id` / `accrued_interest_account_id` — balance-sheet difference in current period.
- **Retained Earnings (Opening)** — P&amp;L portion of prior-period error goes here, never to current-period P&amp;L.

Every row carries `restatement_id` and `restatement_period={YYYY-MM}`.

Financial-statement comparatives re-issuance is a reporting-layer concern — out of scope for this contract.

### 8.5 Command → contract mapping

| A-0044 command | This doc section | Period lock behaviour |
|---|---|---|
| `EditLoanNonPosting` | §8.1 (no GL) | Allowed in any state |
| `ReclassifyCurrentPeriod` | §8.2 | Rejected if target period locked |
| `ChangeLoanEstimate` | §8.3 | Allowed — prospective |
| `RestatePriorPeriod` | §8.4 | Required path when target period is locked |

## 9. Idempotency

Every loan journal carries an idempotency key:

- Origination: `loan:originate:{loan_id}`.
- Accrual run: `loan_accrual_run:{period_end}:{run_uuid}`.
- Per-loan accrual line: `loan_accrual_line:{run_id}:{loan_id}`.
- Payment: `loan:payment:{loan_id}:{payment_date}:{seq}` — `seq` disambiguates multiple payments on the same date.
- Payout: `loan:payout:{loan_id}`.
- Write-off: `loan:writeoff:{loan_id}`.
- Reversal: `loan:reverse:{source_transaction_id}`.

`journal.Post` rejects duplicate idempotency keys with `ErrDuplicateJournal` per A-0009 §Principle 5.

## 10. Tag schema

Every loan-originated `acc_trans` row MUST carry:

- `loan_id` — the loan it belongs to.

Optional tags per event:

- `use_of_funds` — `business` / `mixed` / `private` copied from the loan at origination + accrual + cash-basis payment. Drives R-0073 tax deductibility.
- `run_id` — on per-run accrual rows.
- `payment` / `payout` / `write_off` — boolean event markers for fast queries.
- `correction_id` — on correction reversal + fresh rows (A-0044 `ReclassifyCurrentPeriod`).
- `restatement_id` / `restatement_period` — on prior-period restatement rows.

**Not in the tag set**: `bas_label`. BAS is derived by A-0042 from source tax documents.

## 10a. Closed-period and BAS-lodgement locks

Same enforcement as A-0041 §10a. Every GL-writing command checks `period_lock` and `bas_lodgement` for the target period and rejects with `ErrPeriodLocked` unless the command is `RestatePriorPeriod`. See A-0044 I9.

## 11. Testing requirements

Per A-0009 §Principle 22 (ledger must balance). Table-driven tests for:

- Origination cash with + without establishment fee.
- Origination asset-funded (proceeds debit capital account directly).
- Accrual run across multiple loans including zero-rate loans (must skip / post zero).
- Accrual run period-end rollover across year-end.
- Payment accrual-basis with exact + over + under accrued interest (catch-up accrual triggered).
- Payment cash-basis (no accrual run expected to exist).
- Payment reversal.
- Payout on accrual-basis + on cash-basis; with + without termination fee.
- Payout reversal.
- Write-off — verify `reporting_category='other_income'` on the credit row.
- All four correction commands (§8.1–§8.4).
- Period-lock rejection for every GL-writing command.

Each test asserts:

- Exact accounts, sides, amounts, tags.
- `sum(Dr) == sum(Cr)` for the whole transaction.
- `loan.current_balance` + `accrued_interest_balance` post-event match the ledger.
- Audit row exists with correct actor + diff.

## 12. Observability

Each loan command emits a structured log with:
- `loan_id`, `tenant_id`, `actor_user_id`.
- `command`, `transaction_id`, `idempotency_key`.
- `amounts` (principal, interest, fees).
- `duration_ms`.

Metrics: `loan.originations_total`, `loan.accrual_runs_total`, `loan.payments_total`, `loan.payouts_total`, `loan.write_offs_total`.
