# Summary

Requirements for Australian tax compliance in Ledgius, covering GST classification and calculation, BAS preparation and lodgement, PAYG withholding, PAYG income instalments, STP Phase 2 reporting, ABN validation, tax invoice requirements, and financial year management.

# Problem / Business Context

Australian businesses are legally required to comply with GST, PAYG, superannuation, and STP reporting obligations. Non-compliance results in ATO penalties, interest charges, and potential director liability. Ledgius must correctly classify transactions for GST, calculate BAS figures, withhold tax from employee wages per ATO tax tables, and report payroll data via STP. All calculations must be traceable to authoritative ATO sources.

# Scope

- GST rate structure and classification (Standard 10%, GST-Free, Input-Taxed, Out-of-Scope)
- GST account management (Collected, Paid, Clearing, Adjustments)
- BAS label calculation (G1-G20, 1A, 1B, W1-W5, T1-T9)
- BAS period management (quarterly, monthly, annual)
- PAYG withholding from employee wages per ATO tax tables
- PAYG income instalments (instalment amount and rate methods)
- STP Phase 2 expanded reporting
- ABN validation (11-digit checksum, ABR lookup)
- Tax invoice requirements (8 mandatory elements)
- Financial year management (standard July-June)

# Out of Scope

- International tax compliance (NZ GST, UK VAT, US sales tax)
- Fringe Benefits Tax (FBT) calculation
- Capital Gains Tax (CGT) calculation
- Wine Equalisation Tax (WET) and Luxury Car Tax (LCT)
- ATO electronic lodgement integration (file generation only, not direct submission)
- Tax agent portal integration

# Actors / Users

- Business Owner -- reviews BAS figures, approves lodgement
- Bookkeeper -- classifies transactions for GST, prepares BAS
- Accountant -- verifies tax calculations, reviews PAYG withholding settings
- Payroll Officer -- configures employee tax declarations, processes pay runs with PAYG

# Preconditions

- Chart of Accounts includes GST-specific accounts (Collected, Paid, Clearing, Adjustments)
- Tax codes configured (GST, FRE, INP, EXP, N-T, CAP)
- Financial year set to July-June (or configured substituted accounting period)

# Functional Requirements

## GST Classification

- **AU-TAX-001**: System shall support four GST classifications: Standard (10%), GST-Free (0%), Input-Taxed (N/A), Out-of-Scope (N/A)
- **AU-TAX-002**: System shall calculate GST at 10% on standard-rated supplies
- **AU-TAX-003**: System shall support reverse GST calculation from a GST-inclusive amount (total / 11)
- **AU-TAX-004**: System shall support GST-inclusive to GST-exclusive conversion (total / 1.10)
- **AU-TAX-005**: GST calculations shall use half-up rounding to 2 decimal places
- **AU-TAX-006**: System shall maintain GST accounts: GST Collected (liability), GST Paid (asset), GST Clearing/Settlement, GST Adjustments

## BAS Calculation

- **AU-TAX-007**: System shall calculate all BAS GST labels (G1 through G20, 1A, 1B) per the BAS label reference table in AI-0021
- **AU-TAX-008**: G6 shall be calculated as G1 - G5 (total sales subject to GST)
- **AU-TAX-009**: G9 (GST on sales) shall be calculated as G8 / 11
- **AU-TAX-010**: G17 shall be calculated as G12 - G16 (total purchases subject to GST)
- **AU-TAX-011**: G20 (GST on purchases) shall be calculated as G19 / 11
- **AU-TAX-012**: 1A shall equal G9; 1B shall equal G20
- **AU-TAX-013**: System shall support BAS periods: quarterly (most common for SMBs), monthly (required if GST turnover > $20M or voluntary), annual (voluntary registrants with turnover < $75K)
- **AU-TAX-014**: Quarterly BAS due dates: Q1 (Jul-Sep) 28 Oct, Q2 (Oct-Dec) 28 Feb, Q3 (Jan-Mar) 28 Apr, Q4 (Apr-Jun) 28 Aug

## PAYG Withholding

- **AU-TAX-015**: System shall withhold tax from employee wages per ATO tax tables (Schedule 1 coefficient method)
- **AU-TAX-016**: System shall calculate BAS PAYG labels: W1 (total salary/wages), W2 (amounts withheld from W1), W3 (other amounts withheld), W4 (total withheld = W2 + W3)
- **AU-TAX-017**: Where an employee has not provided a TFN within 28 days, system shall withhold at 47% flat rate with no tax-free threshold

## PAYG Income Instalments

- **AU-TAX-018**: System shall support PAYG income instalments via instalment amount (ATO-provided) or instalment rate method
- **AU-TAX-019**: PAYG instalment labels (T1-T9) shall be calculated on BAS

## STP Reporting

- **AU-TAX-020**: System shall support STP Phase 2 expanded reporting: gross pay, tax withheld, super, allowances, deductions, income type breakdown
- **AU-TAX-021**: STP reporting shall be integrated via SBR (Standard Business Reporting)

## ABN Validation

- **AU-TAX-022**: System shall validate ABN format (11-digit identifier with modulus 89 checksum)
- **AU-TAX-023**: System shall support ABN lookup via ABR (Australian Business Register) web services
- **AU-TAX-024**: ABN shall be required on all tax invoices

## Tax Invoice Requirements

- **AU-TAX-025**: System shall enforce eight mandatory elements on tax invoices: (1) the words "Tax Invoice", (2) seller identity, (3) seller ABN, (4) date of issue, (5) brief description, (6) GST amount, (7) total price including GST, (8) buyer identity/ABN for invoices >= $1,000

## Financial Year

- **AU-TAX-026**: Default financial year shall be 1 July to 30 June
- **AU-TAX-027**: System shall support substituted accounting periods (SAP) for entities that have elected non-standard year-ends

# UX / UI Behaviour

- Tax code selection available on all transaction line items
- BAS preparation page shows calculated labels with drill-down to source transactions
- BAS periods displayed with due dates and lodgement status
- Tax invoice preview validates mandatory elements before sending
- ABN field validates on blur with checksum and optional ABR lookup

# Validation Rules

- GST amount must equal line amount * 0.10 for standard-rated supplies (within rounding tolerance)
- BAS label G6 must equal G1 - G5
- BAS label 1A must equal G9
- ABN must pass modulus 89 checksum
- Tax invoices >= $1,000 must include buyer identity or ABN
- PAYG withholding must use current FY tax tables

# Error Handling

- Invalid GST calculation displays expected vs actual amounts
- BAS labels that do not reconcile display which source figures are inconsistent
- ABN checksum failure displays clear validation message
- Incomplete tax invoice displays which mandatory elements are missing

# Security / Permissions

- BAS preparation requires at least Bookkeeper role
- BAS lodgement approval requires Business Owner or Accountant role
- TFN data encrypted at rest (Privacy Act 1988 compliance)
- PAYG withholding settings require at least Bookkeeper role

# Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] GST calculated correctly at 10% with half-up rounding
- [ ] Reverse GST (total / 11) produces correct results
- [ ] All BAS labels (G1-G20, 1A, 1B) calculated from transaction data
- [ ] BAS quarterly periods with correct due dates
- [ ] PAYG withholding calculated per ATO Schedule 1 coefficient method
- [ ] No-TFN withholding at 47% flat rate
- [ ] ABN validated with modulus 89 checksum
- [ ] Tax invoice validates all 8 mandatory elements
- [ ] Financial year defaults to July-June
- [ ] STP Phase 2 data structure supports expanded reporting fields
