# Summary

Manage customer and vendor contacts with a three-tier entity model: Entity (base identity), Company (business details including ABN), and CreditAccount (AR/AP account linkage, credit terms, currency). Contacts are created atomically across all three tiers and can be filtered by type (customer or vendor).

# Problem / Business Context

Invoices and bills require linked customer/vendor contacts with associated AR/AP accounts. Ledgius must support a structured contact model that stores business details (legal name, ABN), credit terms, and the GL account linkage needed for automatic AR/AP posting.

# Scope

- Contact creation (entity + company + credit account in a single transaction)
- Customer listing and vendor listing (separate endpoints)
- Contact detail retrieval with entity relationship
- Contact status management (active, on_hold, archived) with OPA policy validation
- Audit stamping on credit account mutations

# Out of Scope

- Contact update (editing entity/company/credit account fields after creation)
- Contact deletion
- Contact addresses and phone numbers
- Contact notes/communication history
- Contact merge/deduplication
- Multi-currency per contact (single currency per credit account)

# Actors / Users

- Business Owner -- manages customer and vendor relationships
- Bookkeeper -- creates contacts for invoicing and bill entry
- Accountant -- reviews credit terms and account linkage

# Preconditions

- User is authenticated
- Chart of accounts is configured with AR and AP accounts
- Country reference data is available

# Functional Requirements

- **CON-001**: System shall create a contact atomically across three tiers: Entity (name, country, control_code), Company (legal_name, tax_id/ABN), and CreditAccount (entity_class, meta_number, currency, AR/AP account, credit_limit, terms), all within a single database transaction
- **CON-002**: Entity class shall distinguish vendors (1) from customers (2)
- **CON-003**: System shall list customers (entity_class=2) separately from vendors (entity_class=1) via dedicated endpoints
- **CON-004**: Contact summary shall include: id, name, control_code, meta_number, entity_class, legal_name, tax_id (ABN), curr, credit_limit
- **CON-005**: System shall return contact detail with the entity relationship loaded
- **CON-006**: System shall support updating contact status (active, on_hold, archived), validated via OPA policy `contact_validation`
- **CON-007**: Entity creation shall record created_by and updated_by from the authenticated user context
- **CON-008**: Credit account mutations shall be audit-stamped

# Validation Rules

- Name is required
- Country ID is required
- Legal name is required
- Entity class must be 1 (Vendor) or 2 (Customer)
- Meta number is required
- Currency is required
- Status must be one of: active, on_hold, archived (validated via OPA policy)

# Error Handling

- Invalid entity_class returns validation error
- Invalid status returns error: "invalid status: X (must be active, on_hold, or archived)"
- Database constraint violations (duplicate meta_number) return appropriate errors

2. **Contact deletion**: No delete or deactivation mechanism beyond status change.
3. **Addresses**: No address storage yet.
