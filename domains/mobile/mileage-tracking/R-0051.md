# Summary

ATO-compliant vehicle mileage tracking integrated into the Ledgius mobile app and web platform. Users log business trips (manually or via GPS auto-detection), maintain vehicle records, run 12-week logbook periods, and generate ATO-compliant reports. Mileage data flows into expense claims and tax deduction calculations, supporting both the logbook method and the cents-per-km method.

# Business Context

Australian sole traders, tradespeople, and small business owners who use a vehicle for work need to track business kilometres for tax deductions. The ATO requires either:

1. **Logbook method**: maintain a logbook for a continuous 12-week period recording every trip (business and personal). The resulting business-use percentage is then applied to all vehicle expenses for up to 5 years.
2. **Cents-per-km method**: claim a flat rate per business kilometre (85 cents/km for FY2025, capped at 5,000 km per vehicle per year). No logbook required, but trip records are recommended.

Currently, most users track mileage in a separate app (e.g. Drivers Note, MileIQ) or on paper. This creates disconnected data — the mileage doesn't flow into their accounting system, and their accountant has to manually reconcile logbook data at tax time.

By integrating mileage tracking directly into Ledgius:
- Business trips automatically become claimable expenses
- Fuel receipt captures link to specific vehicles
- The accountant can see logbook data alongside all other financials
- ATO-compliant logbook PDFs generate with one tap
- No need for a separate mileage app

# Core Principle: One-Tap Tracking

The mileage tracker must be as frictionless as possible. Tradespeople are driving between jobs all day — they won't use a feature that requires multiple taps or data entry before each trip. The system should:

- Auto-detect trip start/stop via GPS (opt-in)
- Default to "business" for trips during work hours
- Allow one-tap manual trip logging (enter km only, fill in details later)
- Batch-classify trips at end of day/week

# Acceptance Criteria

## AC-1: Vehicle Register

Maintain a list of vehicles used for business:

- **Add vehicle**: registration number, make, model, year, current odometer reading
- **Multiple vehicles**: support multiple vehicles per business (tradies often have a ute + a car)
- **Default vehicle**: mark one as default for quick trip logging
- **Odometer history**: track odometer readings over time for audit trail
- **Archive**: deactivate vehicles no longer in use (don't delete — keep history)

## AC-2: Manual Trip Logging

Record trips by entering odometer readings or distance:

- **Quick log**: start odometer, end odometer → distance auto-calculated. Or enter distance directly.
- **Trip details**: date, from location, to location, purpose/description
- **Business/personal**: toggle per trip (default: business during Mon-Fri 6am-7pm, personal otherwise)
- **Bulk entry**: enter multiple past trips in one session (catch-up logging)
- **Vehicle selection**: select from registered vehicles (defaults to default vehicle)
- **Round trip**: option to double the distance for return journeys
- **Recurring trips**: mark a trip as recurring (e.g. "Home → Office" daily) for quick repeat logging

## AC-3: GPS Auto-Tracking

Automatically detect and record trips using device GPS (opt-in):

- **Background tracking**: when enabled, monitor device location in the background
- **Trip detection**: detect trip start when speed exceeds ~10 km/h, detect trip end when stationary for >3 minutes
- **Route recording**: store GPS coordinates at intervals (every 30 seconds or 200m, whichever is less frequent) for route display
- **Distance calculation**: calculate total distance from GPS path (more accurate than straight-line)
- **Auto-classify**: trips during configured work hours default to "business", others to "personal"
- **Battery awareness**: reduce GPS frequency when battery is low (<20%); pause tracking when <10%
- **Privacy**: GPS data stored on-device and uploaded to API only on user action. Clear opt-in with explanation of data usage.
- **Saved locations**: learn frequent locations (home, office, client sites) to auto-fill from/to fields

## AC-4: ATO Logbook Method (12-Week Period)

Support the ATO's logbook method for motor vehicle deductions:

- **Start logbook period**: user initiates a 12-week (84-day) logbook period for a vehicle
- **Period tracking**: display progress bar (e.g. "Week 4 of 12 — 38 days remaining")
- **Mandatory completeness**: during the logbook period, ALL trips must be logged (business and personal). Alert if a day has no trips logged.
- **Business-use percentage**: at end of 12-week period, calculate: `business_km / total_km × 100`
- **Period validation**: warn if:
  - Any days have no trips logged
  - The period is not representative (e.g. holiday period)
  - Total km seems low for the period
- **Apply percentage**: the calculated business-use % is applied to all vehicle running costs for the remainder of the FY (and up to 5 years if driving patterns haven't changed)
- **History**: store completed logbook periods with their business-use percentages
- **Re-do**: user can start a new logbook period at any time (e.g. if driving patterns change)

## AC-5: Cents-Per-Km Method

Support the simpler cents-per-km deduction method:

- **ATO rate**: use the current ATO rate (85c/km for FY2025). Rate should be configurable per FY.
- **Cap**: maximum 5,000 business km per vehicle per FY. Warn user when approaching cap.
- **Calculation**: `deduction = min(business_km, 5000) × rate_per_km`
- **No logbook required**: but trip records are still recommended as substantiation
- **Comparison**: show user which method (logbook vs cents-per-km) produces a higher deduction

## AC-6: Fuel Logging

Track fuel purchases linked to vehicles:

- **Log fuel**: date, vehicle, litres, cost, odometer reading, fuel type
- **Receipt link**: link a captured receipt (from the Capture tab) to a fuel entry
- **Fuel economy**: calculate L/100km from fuel logs and odometer readings
- **Cost tracking**: running total of fuel costs per vehicle per period

## AC-7: Reports & Export

Generate ATO-compliant logbook reports:

- **Logbook PDF**: ATO-compliant format showing each trip during the 12-week period — date, start/end odometer, from/to, purpose, business/personal, distance
- **FY summary**: total business km, total personal km, business-use %, deduction amount (both methods), vehicle running costs
- **Trip export**: CSV export of all trips for a date range
- **Accountant view**: tax accountant can view logbook data for all client vehicles in the web app
- **Method comparison**: side-by-side comparison of logbook method vs cents-per-km deduction

## AC-8: Expense Integration

Connect mileage data to the general ledger:

- **Auto-expense (logbook method)**: when vehicle running costs are recorded (fuel, rego, insurance, repairs), apply the business-use % and create a deductible expense entry
- **Auto-expense (cents-per-km)**: at end of period, create an expense journal entry for the calculated deduction
- **GL account**: map to a configurable "Motor Vehicle Expenses" GL account
- **BAS impact**: motor vehicle expenses flow into BAS preparation where GST credits apply (e.g. fuel with GST)

# User Interface

## Mobile App

- **Mileage screen**: accessible from Home tab quick actions or as a dedicated section
- **Active trip banner**: when GPS tracking is active, show a persistent banner at top of app with current trip distance and elapsed time
- **Trip list**: scrollable list of recent trips with business/personal indicators, grouped by day
- **Quick log FAB**: floating button to log a trip with minimal taps
- **Vehicle picker**: swipe between vehicles at top of screen

## Web App

- **Logbook page** (`/logbook`): under a new "Expenses" or existing "Banking" sidebar section
- **Trip table**: full trip history with filters (date range, vehicle, business/personal)
- **Vehicle management**: add/edit/archive vehicles
- **Logbook periods**: start/view/complete 12-week logbook periods
- **Dashboard widget**: business-use % gauge, YTD business km, deduction estimate
- **PDF export**: generate and download ATO-compliant logbook PDF

# ATO Compliance References

- [Motor vehicle and car expenses (ATO)](https://www.ato.gov.au/individuals-and-families/income-deductions-offsets-and-records/deductions-you-can-claim/vehicles-trips-and-travel)
- **Logbook method**: must cover a minimum continuous 12-week period; must record ALL trips (business and personal); logbook is valid for 5 years unless circumstances change
- **Cents-per-km rate**: set annually by the ATO. FY2024: 85c/km. FY2025: 85c/km. Maximum 5,000 business km per vehicle per year.
- **Record keeping**: trip records should include date, start/end odometer, start/end location, purpose, and km travelled
- **Substantiation**: the ATO may request logbook records during an audit; digital records are accepted

# Out of Scope (V1)

- Automatic toll tracking / toll receipt integration
- Fleet management (multiple drivers per vehicle)
- Depreciation calculation for vehicles (separate feature)
- FBT (Fringe Benefits Tax) calculations for employer-provided vehicles
- Integration with vehicle telematics / OBD-II devices
- CarPlay / Android Auto integration
- Geofencing alerts (e.g. "you arrived at a client — log this trip?")
