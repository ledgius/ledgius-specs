# Summary

Extend the Ledgius Knowledge Pipeline (R-0008 / A-0020) to ingest and govern **Australian workplace-relations authority** so that every payroll calculation, eligibility check, and entitlement determination is traceable to a source clause in the **Fair Work Act 2009**, the **National Employment Standards (NES)**, the **122 modern awards**, an **Enterprise Agreement (EBA)** registered with the Fair Work Commission, or an **Individual Flexibility Arrangement (IFA)** captured at the tenant.

Authority artifacts under this pipeline produce reviewable, executable rules — **CEL** (calculations + per-pay-period derivations), **OPA Rego** (eligibility + classification policies), and **go-rules JSON** (decision tables for award-clause selection) — bundled under the regional path `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/` and surfaced as policy articles under `docs/authority/articles/au/payroll/`.

This pipeline is the workplace-relations counterpart to the tax/accounting pipeline already covered by R-0008. The two pipelines share infrastructure, schema disciplines, and review governance, but operate over disjoint source registries, taxonomies, and output bundles.

R-0073 + A-0046 + T-0041 cover **Area 1 (Specs)** and define the contracts that Areas 2, 3, and 4 satisfy:

- **Area 2** is partly delivered by T-0041 (initial 5 awards + NES) and continued in follow-on tasks (Phase 2 expansion to ~30 awards; Phase 3 full 122-award coverage per A-0046 §Migration / Rollout).
- **Area 3** reuses A-0020's existing pipeline machinery — adapters per A-0046 §Tiered Acquisition (FWC awards, FWC Pay Database, FWO NES, Fair Work Act). T-0041 ships the adapters.
- **Area 4** has existing R-specs (R-0002 employees, R-0004 / R-0055 pay runs, R-0005 STP, R-0006 SG, R-0007 PAYG) — those describe the operator UX/API. This pipeline does **not** redefine that surface; it provides the rule bundles + tenant-scoped EBA/IFA model that those existing surfaces consume. New UX surfaces this pipeline introduces are scoped narrowly: tenant-onboarding "Award" step, EBA upload flow, IFA capture form, help-panel award badging.

The four areas progress at different cadences: Area 1 (specs) lands once per significant scope change; Area 2 (rule bundles) lands per award; Area 3 (ingestion) runs continuously; Area 4 (operator UX) follows the existing payroll-feature roadmap. **Area 3 is what makes Area 2 sustainable** — without continuous ingestion, the rule bundles drift the moment the first award variation publishes.

# Problem / Business Context

The existing payroll engine (R-0004 / R-0055 / R-0007 / R-0006) already computes gross pay, PAYG, super, and net pay using a small set of formulas with handwritten authority strings (e.g. `"ATO Schedule 1"`, `"Employment contract"`). That coverage is sufficient for the **statutory federal layer** (PAYG, SG, STP) but does not address the **workplace-relations layer** that determines:

- **Which award applies** to an employee classification (tied to the employer's industry + the role's classification level).
- **Ordinary hours** vs overtime thresholds — vary per award (e.g. 38 ordinary hours/week is the NES baseline; awards alter the spread of hours).
- **Penalty rates** — Saturday, Sunday, public holiday, early-morning, late-night, and shift loadings vary materially per award, and within an award per classification.
- **Allowances** — meal, travel, tool, first-aid, leading-hand, higher-duties, uniform — defined per award; many CPI-indexed annually by Fair Work Commission.
- **Leave entitlements above the NES floor** — many awards add personal/carer's leave or RDO accrual.
- **EBA overrides** — a registered Enterprise Agreement supersedes the underlying award provided the agreement passes the Better Off Overall Test (BOOT). Tenants on an EBA need their EBA terms encoded as overrides on top of the base award.
- **IFA variations** — individual employee variations to award/EBA terms, captured per-employee, must be applied at pay-time.

Without an authority-linked engine for these, the payroll system either (a) becomes a free-text data-entry tool — no compliance gate — or (b) hard-codes one tenant's interpretation — wrong for the next tenant. Either failure mode exposes the operator (and Ledgius) to **underpayment claims**, the dominant compliance risk in Australian payroll.

The Australian regulator landscape is concrete and well-published:

| Authority | Role | Primary surface |
|---|---|---|
| **Fair Work Act 2009** | Statute — the legal foundation for the NES, modern awards, EBAs, IFAs, unfair dismissal, etc. | `legislation.gov.au` (Cth) |
| **Fair Work Ombudsman (FWO)** | Plain-English guidance, "Find My Award" tool, fact sheets, pay calculators | `fairwork.gov.au` |
| **Fair Work Commission (FWC)** | Quasi-judicial body — registers EBAs, varies awards, publishes the Modern Awards Pay Database, hears unfair dismissal | `fwc.gov.au` |
| **State long-service-leave authorities** | LSL portability schemes (BUSSQ for construction in QLD, CoINVEST in VIC, etc.) | Per-state | 

This pipeline ingests from FWO and FWC primarily, with Fair Work Act sections cited for foundational provisions, and state LSL schemes deferred to a future tranche.

# Product Vision — Tier-aware Help for Payroll

The Help Capability Tiers framework (R-0008 §Product Vision) applies here. Payroll is where guided + anticipatory help create the highest leverage:

- **Tier 1 — Reference**: per-page articles already covered by the Frontend-help migration (T-0038).
- **Tier 2 — Contextual**: payroll articles must vary by tenant award + employee classification. A coffee-shop tenant on the *Restaurant Industry Award 2020* should see different content than a tradie tenant on the *Building & Construction General On-site Award 2020*.
- **Tier 3 — Primary + Related**: each pay-run page surfaces *the* applicable award clause for the line being edited (primary), with NES + Act references in the Related accordion.
- **Tier 4 — Operational status**: pay-run readiness checks (no employees missing classification, no allowances missing for the period, no overtime un-approved).
- **Tier 5 — Guided** + **Tier 6 — Anticipatory**: a payroll officer who is *told* "Sarah worked 42 hours this week — 4 hours of overtime under MA000003 clause 21.1, ~$XYZ extra" — without having to look it up — finishes the pay run faster and with materially less underpayment risk than one who relies on the operator's award knowledge.

This requirements doc enables tiers 1–4 directly. Tiers 5–6 build on the artifacts produced here and are tracked separately.

# Scope

- Source registry for AU workplace-relations authority: Fair Work Act 2009, NES, modern awards (initial set ~5–10, scaling to all 122), Fair Work Commission decisions, FWO guidance.
- Knowledge article + passage model adapted for **clause-addressable awards** — every award clause is independently citable.
- **Award classification taxonomy** — every award has its own classification ladder (Level 1 / Level 2 / etc.); taxonomy must accommodate per-award classification axes.
- Synthesised rule artifacts under `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/`:
  - **CEL**: ordinary-hour calculations, penalty-rate computations, allowance values, leave-accrual rates, casual loading.
  - **OPA Rego**: award-applicability decisions ("does award X apply to this employee?"), eligibility (e.g. "is this employee eligible for the leading-hand allowance?"), classification matching.
  - **go-rules JSON**: decision tables for time-of-day → penalty-rate selection, classification → base-rate selection.
- **EBA model** — registered Enterprise Agreements stored as tenant-scoped articles + override rule sets that supersede base award rules where BOOT-tested.
- **IFA model** — per-employee variations as fine-grained overrides at pay-calculation time.
- Bidirectional linking: every CEL/Rego/go-rules artifact links to the award clause(s) it implements; every clause carries `implemented_by` reverse links.
- Help-panel surfacing: payroll pages (`/employees`, `/pay-runs`, `/employees/:id`, the pay-run editor) resolve relevant award + NES articles automatically.
- Build-time validation that every payroll calculation in the engine carries an `authority_refs` link.

# Out of Scope

- **Long-service leave portability schemes** — state-specific (BUSSQ, CoINVEST, etc.) — deferred to a future task.
- **Unfair-dismissal procedural rules** — outside the calculation engine; a help-only concern handled by reference articles, not executable rules.
- **Workers' compensation / icare / WorkSafe** — separate insurance domain, not payroll.
- **NZ / UK payroll authority** — schema is jurisdiction-extensible per R-0008 §KNW-PIP-027; populating non-AU content is a separate effort.
- **Auto-classification of employees** — the system surfaces the applicable clause once classification is set; choosing the right classification for a given role is the operator's responsibility (with help-panel guidance).
- **Award-tool replacement** — Ledgius will not duplicate the FWO "Find My Award" decision tree at v1; we link out and let the operator declare the award.

# Actors / Users

- **Payroll officer** — runs pay each cycle; needs the right award clause surfaced per line.
- **Bookkeeper / accountant** — verifies pay-run output for compliance; needs traceability back to the clause that drove each amount.
- **Business owner** — sets up the tenant once, declares the applicable award (or uploads an EBA), needs clear guidance on which award.
- **Auditor / FWO inspector** — must be able to trace any payroll line back to an award clause + Fair Work Act section.
- **Employee (read-only)** — payslip-level transparency; "this $X allowance comes from clause 21.5 of MA000003".

# Preconditions

- R-0008 knowledge pipeline shipped (article model, page resolver, help panel) — done.
- Existing payroll engine (R-0007 PAYG, R-0006 SG, R-0004/R-0055 pay runs) operational.
- Rules orchestrator (`pkg/rules`) supports per-bundle loading and authority introspection.

# Functional Requirements

## Source Registry

- **PAY-AUTH-001**: System shall maintain a registry of AU workplace-relations source systems: Fair Work Act 2009, NES (FWO articulation), Fair Work Ombudsman, Fair Work Commission award database, Fair Work Commission Modern Awards Pay Database, Fair Work Commission EBA register.
- **PAY-AUTH-002**: Each source shall record: name, authority level (statute / regulator-administered-instrument / regulator-guidance), base URL, access mode (direct PDF, server-rendered HTML, JS-rendered), crawl policy, refresh cadence (Modern Awards: annual on 1 July; Pay Database: monthly).

## Article + Passage Model

- **PAY-AUTH-003**: Modern awards shall be stored as knowledge articles with passage-level addressability matching the award's clause structure (e.g. clause 4 — coverage; clause 13 — classifications; clause 21 — penalties). Each clause becomes a citable passage.
- **PAY-AUTH-004**: NES entitlements shall be stored as 11 articles (one per minimum entitlement: maximum weekly hours, requests for flexible working arrangements, parental leave, annual leave, personal/carer's leave & compassionate leave, community service leave, long service leave, public holidays, notice of termination & redundancy pay, Fair Work Information Statement, casual conversion).
- **PAY-AUTH-005**: Articles shall record award identity (FWC code: e.g. `MA000003` for the Hospitality Industry (General) Award 2020) plus human-readable name plus operative date plus latest variation date.
- **PAY-AUTH-006**: Article filesystem path shall follow the regional pattern: `docs/authority/articles/au/payroll/<source>/<article-id>.yaml` (e.g. `au/payroll/fwc/MA000003.yaml`, `au/payroll/fwo/nes-annual-leave.yaml`).

## Award Classification Taxonomy

- **PAY-AUTH-007**: Taxonomy shall accommodate per-award classification ladders. Canonical key form: `payroll.award.<award_code>.classification.<level>` and `payroll.award.<award_code>.clause.<clause_number>`.
- **PAY-AUTH-008**: Cross-award axes (penalty kind, leave kind, allowance kind) shall use canonical keys independent of any one award: `payroll.penalty_kind.{saturday, sunday, public_holiday, early_morning, late_night, shift_loading}`, `payroll.allowance_kind.{meal, travel, tool, leading_hand, first_aid, higher_duties, uniform_laundry}`, `payroll.leave_kind.{annual, personal_carer, compassionate, long_service, community_service, parental, public_holiday}`.

## Schema Bindings

- **PAY-AUTH-009**: System shall extend the schema registry with payroll-specific entities: `EmployeeClassification` (award_code, level, year_progression), `WorkPattern` (ordinary hours, spread of hours, RDO cycle), `PayPeriod`, `Roster`, `PayItem`, `AllowanceType`, `PenaltyRate`.
- **PAY-AUTH-010**: Every CEL/Rego/go-rules artifact in the payroll bundle shall bind to a specific schema version of these entities. No free-text field paths.

## Synthesised Artifacts

- **PAY-AUTH-011**: Each award shall produce, where applicable: (a) a CEL formula set for ordinary-hour pay + casual loading + penalty rates per relevant penalty kind + each allowance value; (b) an OPA Rego policy for award applicability + classification eligibility + leading-hand-style flag eligibility; (c) go-rules decision tables for time-of-day → penalty selection + classification → base-rate selection.
- **PAY-AUTH-012**: Each award shall be defined in **its own dedicated set of files** — never co-mingled with other awards in a shared bundle. Files are semantically named and numbered using the FWC code as the stable identifier:
  - CEL formulas: `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/<MA-code>_<short-slug>_v<semver>.yaml` (e.g. `MA000003_hospitality_v2026.7.1.yaml`)
  - OPA Rego policy: `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/<MA-code>_<short-slug>.rego` (e.g. `MA000003_hospitality.rego`)
  - go-rules decision tables: `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/<MA-code>_<short-slug>_decisions.json`
  Per-award separation is non-negotiable: one award's variation does not invalidate the bundle of an unaffected award; one award's annual update does not require re-publishing every payroll bundle. A-0046 documents the layout in full.
- **PAY-AUTH-013**: NES entitlements shall produce a cross-award baseline bundle: `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/nes_v<semver>.yaml` (CEL leave-accrual rates) plus `nes.rego` (eligibility) — the floor below which no award/EBA may go.

## EBA Modelling

- **PAY-AUTH-014**: A tenant on a registered EBA shall declare the EBA at tenant-onboarding time (FWC EBA reference + operative date + nominal expiry date).
- **PAY-AUTH-015**: EBAs shall be modelled as **override bundles** layered over the base award: same artifact shape (CEL + Rego + go-rules) but tenant-scoped and applied with higher precedence at runtime.
- **PAY-AUTH-016**: The runtime resolver shall apply: NES floor → award → EBA override → IFA override, in that order. Any layer may impose a higher entitlement than the layer below; no layer may impose a lower entitlement than the NES floor.
- **PAY-AUTH-017**: EBA expiry tracking — the system shall warn the tenant 60 days before nominal expiry, and refuse to silently apply an expired EBA without an explicit "rolled-over" declaration.

## IFA Modelling

- **PAY-AUTH-018**: An IFA shall be captured per-employee with: parties, date, varied clauses, varied terms, BOOT-passing declaration.
- **PAY-AUTH-019**: IFAs shall be applied at pay-calculation time as a fine-grained override on the resolved award/EBA layer for that employee only.
- **PAY-AUTH-020**: IFAs shall be terminable by the employee with 13 weeks' notice per Fair Work Act s203(6); the system shall track active vs terminated state.

## Help Panel Surfacing

- **PAY-AUTH-021**: Payroll pages (`/employees`, `/employees/:id`, `/pay-runs`, `/pay-runs/:id`, `/payg-config`, `/super-rates`, `/super-obligations`) shall resolve relevant articles via the existing `usePagePolicies` mechanism with appropriate domain tags (`payroll`, `award`, `nes`).
- **PAY-AUTH-022**: When a tenant has declared an applicable award, that award's article shall surface as the **primary** policy on payroll pages; NES + Fair Work Act articles surface in the Related accordion.
- **PAY-AUTH-023**: Help panel shall visually distinguish FWC + FWO authority badges from ATO + AASB authority badges (existing badge system per R-0008 §KNW-PIP-022 — extends to FWC/FWO).

## Authority Coverage Validation

- **PAY-AUTH-024**: Every CEL formula, OPA policy, and go-rules decision under `pkg/rules/bundles/au/payroll/` shall carry `authority_refs` to at least one published article (award clause, NES section, or Fair Work Act section). Build-time CI shall fail on missing or broken references.
- **PAY-AUTH-025**: The existing AI-0023 authority coverage audit shall be extended to report per-award coverage % and flag awards with <80% formula coverage as draft-only (not runtime-eligible).

## Non-Functional Requirements

- **PAY-AUTH-026**: Article + bundle loading shall not regress existing knowledge-pipeline P95 latency (<200ms per page).
- **PAY-AUTH-027**: Award updates (FWC publishes annual variations 1 July) shall be rolled out via a dated bundle version (`MA000003_v2026.7.1.yaml`); old versions stay in the bundle for historical pay-runs to remain reproducible.
- **PAY-AUTH-028**: Award PDF + HTML raw sources shall be retained for audit per the existing R-0008 §KNW-PIP-005 raw-source requirement.

# UX / UI Behaviour

- **Tenant onboarding** gains an "Award" step: declare applicable award via FWC code or via a search picker; if the tenant is on an EBA, additional EBA-upload step.
- **Employee detail** page surfaces the resolved (NES → Award → EBA → IFA) entitlement set for the employee, with each line citing the source layer.
- **Pay-run editor** surfaces per-line clause references when an unusual rate applies (e.g. a Sunday penalty); operator can click through to the clause text in the help panel.
- **Help panel** for payroll pages renders FWC / FWO / Fair Work Act badges alongside the existing ATO / AASB / Ledgius badges (extension of R-0008 §KNW-PIP-022 colour scheme — propose: orange for FWC, teal for FWO, dark blue for Fair Work Act).

# Validation Rules

- Every published payroll bundle artifact must reference at least one award/NES/Act passage.
- An EBA cannot be applied to a tenant without a declared base award.
- An IFA cannot be applied to an employee without a declared classification under the applicable award.
- BOOT-passing declaration is a required tick at EBA-upload + IFA-creation time; the system records who ticked it and when.
- Award bundles flagged `<80%` formula coverage in AI-0023 are draft-only and produce a UI warning when applied at runtime.

# Error Handling

- Missing classification on an employee → pay-run dry-run fails with a specific error: "Employee X has no classification declared under MA000003".
- EBA expired without rolled-over declaration → pay-run dry-run warns; tenant must explicitly extend or remove the EBA.
- Award PDF/HTML refresh detects content hash change → article goes into "stale-pending-review" state per R-0008 §KNW-PIP-015.

# Security / Permissions

- Knowledge articles are read-only via the public knowledge endpoint (per R-0008 §KNW-PIP-027).
- EBA + IFA tenant data are tenant-scoped and access-controlled like any other tenant data (per existing platform RBAC).
- Raw award PDFs retained in version control for audit; not exposed via API.

# Open Questions

1. **Scope of v1 award set** — recommend starting with: Clerks Private Sector Award (MA000002), Hospitality (MA000003), General Retail Industry Award (MA000004), Building & Construction General On-site Award (MA000020), Professional Employees Award (MA000065). Five awards covers a meaningful share of small-business tenants and exercises the diverse-clause-shape problem before scaling.
2. **PDF vs HTML primary source** — FWC publishes both. PDF is canonical for audit; HTML is more practical for ingestion. Recommend HTML as primary, PDF as retained raw, with HTML→article extraction reviewed against the PDF.
3. **EBA storage location** — tenant-scoped data but knowledge-article-shaped. Two options: (a) store as articles under a tenant-scoped path (e.g. `docs/authority/tenants/<tenant_id>/eba/...`), or (b) store as a tenant DB record with article-shape JSON. Recommend (b) — tenant data shouldn't ride in the source-controlled article store.
4. **Annual award updates** — FWC varies most awards annually on 1 July. Need an automated detection + draft-PR generation flow, or manual quarterly review + bulk update? Recommend automated detection + manual review for v1, with the goal of fully automated by v2.
5. **Pay Database integration** — the FWC Modern Awards Pay Database has structured wage data. Worth treating as a first-class structured source (Tier-1 acquisition per A-0046) rather than HTML-scraping the award PDFs for rate tables. Add to source registry as a separate adapter.
