# Summary

Calculate and manage superannuation guarantee (SG) contributions for employees. Store SG rates per financial year, calculate SG on Ordinary Time Earnings during pay runs, track employee super fund details, enforce maximum quarterly contribution base caps, and prepare data for SuperStream contributions.

# Problem / Business Context

The Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992 mandates that Australian employers contribute a minimum percentage of each employee's Ordinary Time Earnings (OTE) to their nominated superannuation fund. The SG rate increases annually per a legislated schedule (11.5% FY2024-25, 12% FY2025-26). Contributions must be paid quarterly by the 28th of the month following each quarter. Failure to meet obligations results in the Superannuation Guarantee Charge (SGC), which includes interest and administrative penalties. Employers must also use SuperStream for electronic contributions.

# Scope

- SG rate storage per financial year with maximum quarterly contribution base
- SG calculation on OTE during pay run processing
- Employee super fund detail management (fund name, ABN, USI, member number)
- Maximum quarterly contribution base cap enforcement
- Salary sacrifice super contributions
- Quarterly SG obligation tracking and payment status
- SuperStream-compatible contribution data generation
- SG rate display in frontend

# Out of Scope

- SuperStream gateway certification (use clearing house for initial release)
- Super choice form processing and compliance
- Self-managed super fund (SMSF) validation
- Defined benefit fund calculations
- Co-contribution and spouse contribution processing
- Super guarantee charge (SGC) penalty calculations
- Voluntary employer super contributions beyond SG

# Actors / Users

- Business Owner -- reviews super obligations, ensures quarterly payments are made
- Bookkeeper -- verifies SG calculations in pay runs, manages employee super fund details
- Accountant -- reconciles quarterly SG liabilities, verifies compliance
- Super Fund -- receives contributions via SuperStream

# Preconditions

- Employee records exist with super fund details (fund name, ABN, USI, member number)
- SG rates loaded for the relevant financial year
- Pay run processing is operational
- Chart of Accounts includes super payable liability account

# Functional Requirements

- **PAY-SUP-001**: System shall calculate SG on Ordinary Time Earnings (OTE) only; OTE excludes overtime but includes some allowances, with classification configurable per pay component
- **PAY-SUP-002**: System shall use the correct SG rate based on the pay date's financial year
- **PAY-SUP-003**: System shall store SG rates per financial year with the maximum quarterly contribution base
- **PAY-SUP-004**: System shall cap SG calculation at the maximum quarterly contribution base (e.g. FY2025-26 max quarterly base is $67,500)
- **PAY-SUP-005**: System shall support salary sacrifice super contributions (pre-tax additional super on top of SG)
- **PAY-SUP-006**: System shall track super fund details per employee (fund name, ABN, USI, member number)
- **PAY-SUP-007**: System shall generate SuperStream-compatible contribution data for transmission via clearing house
- **PAY-SUP-008**: System shall track quarterly SG obligations and payment status per employee
- **PAY-SUP-009**: System shall warn when a quarterly SG contribution deadline is approaching (28th of month after quarter end)
- **PAY-SUP-010**: System shall handle super for employees under 18 years of age (SG only applies if working more than 30 hours per week)

# Data / Entities / Fields

Primary entity: `SuperGuaranteeRate`

| Field | Type | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| id | int (PK) | Auto-increment |
| rate | decimal(5,4) | e.g. 0.1200 = 12% |
| effective_from | date | Start of financial year |
| effective_to | date (nullable) | End of financial year |
| max_quarterly_base | decimal (nullable) | Max OTE per quarter for SG cap |

Seed data: 6 rows covering FY 2020-21 (9.5%) through FY 2025-26 (12.0%)

Employee super fields (on Employee entity per R-0002): super_fund_name, super_fund_abn, super_fund_usi, super_member_number

# UX / UI Behaviour

- Super rates page displays all financial year rates with effective dates and max quarterly base
- FY labels shown alongside rates for clarity (e.g. "FY 2025-26: 12.00%")
- Employee detail page includes super fund details section (editable in draft, read-only when locked)
- Quarterly SG obligation summary available under reporting
- Approaching deadline warnings displayed as alerts in app header

# Validation Rules

- SG rate must be positive and less than 1.0 (expressed as decimal, e.g. 0.12)
- Effective dates must not overlap for the same rate type
- Employee super fund ABN must be 11 digits if provided
- USI must follow the standard format if provided
- OTE must be non-negative

# Error Handling

- Missing SG rate for the pay date's financial year returns error with the date
- Missing employee super fund details returns warning (SG still calculated, but contribution cannot be remitted)
- Quarterly base cap exceeded returns informational message -- calculation proceeds with capped amount

# Security / Permissions

- Minimum role: Bookkeeper (view rates, manage employee super details)
- Super fund details (ABN, USI, member number) visible to authorised users only
- SG calculations audit-logged as part of pay run audit trail

# Regulatory References

- Superannuation Guarantee (Administration) Act 1992: Mandates employer SG contributions on OTE
- SG Rate Schedule: 9.5% (FY2020-21) -> 10% (FY2021-22) -> 10.5% (FY2022-23) -> 11% (FY2023-24) -> 11.5% (FY2024-25) -> 12% (FY2025-26)
- SuperStream: Electronic standard for super contributions, required for all employers
- ATO Quarterly Deadlines: Contributions due by 28th of month following each quarter (28 Oct, 28 Jan, 28 Apr, 28 Jul)
- Maximum Quarterly Contribution Base: Indexed annually by ATO

# Open Questions

1. **SuperStream compliance**: Direct SuperStream integration is complex. Recommendation is to use the ATO Small Business Super Clearing House or a commercial clearing house as an intermediary for initial release.
2. **OTE definition**: What constitutes OTE varies by award and employment agreement. May need configurable rules per employee or award to determine which pay components are OTE.
3. **Salary sacrifice caps**: Concessional contribution caps ($30,000 pa) need to be tracked to avoid excess contributions. This may require integration with employee-level contribution tracking.
