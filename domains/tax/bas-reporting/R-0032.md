# Summary

Generate Australian Business Activity Statement (BAS) reports for a given period, including all GST labels (G1-G20), PAYG withholding stubs (W1-W4), and a net GST/PAYG position summary. Also provides transaction-level GST detail reports broken down by sales and purchases.

# Problem / Business Context

Australian businesses registered for GST must lodge a BAS with the ATO (quarterly or monthly) reporting GST collected on sales, GST paid on purchases, and PAYG withholding. Ledgius must calculate these values from the general ledger and present them in the standard BAS label format.

# Scope

- BAS report generation for a date range (G1-G20 GST labels, W1-W4 PAYG stubs, summary)
- GST detail report with transaction-level breakdown (sales and purchases separately)
- Period label formatting (quarterly FY format, monthly format, custom range)
- GST calculations via CEL rules engine (bas_gst_on_sales, bas_gst_on_purchases, bas_net_gst)

# Out of Scope

- PAYG withholding actual calculations (W1-W4 are zero stubs pending payroll implementation)
- BAS lodgement to ATO
- BAS amendment/revision tracking
- GST-free, input-taxed, and export sales classification (all sales currently treated as standard-rated)
- Capital vs non-capital purchase classification
- BAS instalment amount calculation (T-series labels)

# Actors / Users

- Business Owner -- reviews BAS before lodgement
- Accountant -- generates BAS for quarterly/monthly lodgement
- Bookkeeper -- reviews GST detail to verify transactions

# Preconditions

- User is authenticated
- Transactions exist in the period with GST entries
- GST Collected (accno 2200) and GST Paid (accno 1200) accounts exist

# Functional Requirements

- **BAS-001**: System shall generate a BAS report for a given from_date to to_date period
- **BAS-002**: G1 (Total sales) shall be calculated as total sales income plus GST collected
- **BAS-003**: G5 (GST-free + export + input-taxed) shall be calculated as G2 + G3 + G4 (currently zero)
- **BAS-004**: G6 (Sales subject to GST) shall be G1 - G5
- **BAS-005**: G8 (After adjustments) shall be G6 + G7 (G7 adjustments currently zero)
- **BAS-006**: G9 (GST on sales) shall be calculated via CEL rule `bas_gst_on_sales`
- **BAS-007**: G11 (Non-capital purchases) shall be total purchases plus GST paid (all purchases treated as non-capital)
- **BAS-008**: G12 through G19 shall follow the standard BAS calculation chain
- **BAS-009**: G20 (GST on purchases) shall be calculated via CEL rule `bas_gst_on_purchases`
- **BAS-010**: Label 1A (GST on sales) shall be actual GST collected from the ledger
- **BAS-011**: Label 1B (GST on purchases) shall be actual GST paid from the ledger
- **BAS-012**: GST owed shall be calculated via CEL rule `bas_net_gst` (1A - 1B)
- **BAS-013**: Total owed shall be GST owed + PAYG owed
- **BAS-014**: Period label shall be auto-formatted: "Q1 FY2026" for quarters, "Jan 2026" for months, "DD Mon YYYY to DD Mon YYYY" for custom ranges
- **BAS-015**: System shall provide transaction-level GST detail with separate sales and purchases lists, each showing trans_id, transdate, reference, description, net_amount, gst_amount, gross_amount, trans_type, contact_name

# Validation Rules

- from_date and to_date are required (ISO 8601 format)
- from_date must be before to_date

# Error Handling

- Missing from_date or to_date returns 400
- Database errors in GST queries return 500 with wrapped error context

# Open Questions

1. **GST-free/export/input-taxed classification**: All sales are currently treated as standard-rated; G2, G3, G4 are zero.
2. **Capital vs non-capital purchases**: All purchases treated as non-capital (G10 always zero).
3. **PAYG withholding**: W1-W4 are stubs pending payroll implementation.
4. **BAS adjustments**: G7 and G18 adjustment fields are not yet populated.
